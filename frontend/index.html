Did You Know?

🌬️

Natural Air Conditioners

A single large tree can transpire up to 400 litres of water a day, cooling the surrounding air — the equivalent of five average room air-conditioners running for 20 hours.

👴

Ancient Survivors

Methuselah, a Great Basin Bristlecone Pine in California, is approximately 5,000 years old — making it the world's oldest known non-clonal living tree. Its exact location is kept secret to protect it.

🔗

The Underground Wood Wide Web

Trees communicate and share nutrients through vast fungal networks called mycorrhizae. Mother trees can recognise their own kin and preferentially send carbon and nutrients to their seedlings.

💧

Watershed Guardians

A mature forest can absorb up to 18 inches of rainfall before water reaches streams, dramatically reducing flood risk and replenishing underground aquifers.

🏔️

Sky-Scraping Giants

Hyperion, a Coast Redwood in northern California, stands 115.92 m (380.3 ft) tall — taller than the Statue of Liberty including its pedestal. It was discovered in 2006 deep in a national park.

🌡️

Temperature Controllers

Streets lined with trees can be up to 10 °C cooler than treeless streets in summer, reducing the urban heat island effect and cutting air-conditioning energy use by 30 %.

🫁

Oxygen Factories

One large tree produces enough oxygen for two people to breathe for an entire year, while simultaneously locking away carbon that would otherwise contribute to climate change.

🪓

Resilient Regrowth

The Pando Aspen grove in Utah is considered one organism — a clonal colony of around 47,000 genetically identical trees sharing a single root system weighing an estimated 6 million kg.
